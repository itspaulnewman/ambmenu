Filipino Pork Siomai: A Delicious Steamed Dumpling Recipe

Filipino Pork Siomai is a delightful and savory dumpling that has become a favorite among many food lovers. Originating from Chinese cuisine, siomai has been adapted to suit Filipino tastes, incorporating local ingredients and flavors that make it unique. These steamed dumplings are not only delicious but also versatile, making them a popular choice for snacks, appetizers, or even main dishes. In this article, we will explore the history of Filipino Pork Siomai, provide a detailed recipe, and share tips for perfecting this beloved dish.

What is Filipino Pork Siomai?

Siomai is a type of traditional dumpling that is typically filled with ground meat and vegetables. In the Philippines, Pork Siomai is particularly popular and is often served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce and calamansi. The dumplings are usually wrapped in wonton or siomai wrappers and steamed to perfection. The combination of pork, shrimp, and vegetables creates a flavorful filling that is both satisfying and comforting.

Key Ingredients for Filipino Pork Siomai

To prepare authentic Filipino Pork Siomai, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • ½ cup shrimp (peeled and finely chopped)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 small carrot (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup jicama (finely chopped, optional)
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 30-40 siomai or wonton wrappers

Step-by-Step Recipe for Filipino Pork Siomai

Preparation Time:

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 20 minutes
  • Total Time: 40 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, onion, garlic, carrot, jicama (if using), beaten egg, sesame oil, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are thoroughly combined.
  2. Wrap the Siomai:Take a siomai wrapper and place about 1 tablespoon of the filling in the center. Gather the edges of the wrapper around the filling to form a cup shape while leaving the top open. Repeat this process until all the filling is used.
  3. Prepare the Steamer:Fill your steamer with water and bring it to a boil. If using a metal steamer basket, line it with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking.
  4. Steam the Siomai:Arrange the wrapped siomai in the steamer basket in a single layer, making sure they don't touch each other. Cover and steam for about 15-20 minutes or until cooked through.
  5. Serve:Once cooked, carefully remove the siomai from the steamer and transfer them to a serving plate. Serve hot with soy sauce mixed with calamansi juice for dipping.

Variations of Filipino Pork Siomai

Seafood Siomai

For seafood lovers:

  • Substitute some or all of the pork with minced fish or additional shrimp to create a delicious seafood version of siomai.

Vegetarian Siomai

For a meat-free option:

  • Replace pork with finely chopped mushrooms, tofu, and vegetables like carrots and cabbage for a tasty vegetarian alternative.

Spicy Siomai

If you enjoy heat:

  • Add chili flakes or finely chopped chili peppers to the filling mixture for an extra kick.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I use frozen siomai wrappers?Yes! Frozen siomai or wonton wrappers are convenient and can be used directly from the freezer without thawing.

How do I store leftover siomai?Store any leftover siomai in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat by steaming or microwaving before serving.

Can I freeze uncooked siomai?Absolutely! You can freeze uncooked siomai by placing them on a baking sheet until frozen solid before transferring them to a freezer bag. Steam from frozen when ready to cook.

What can I serve with Filipino Pork Siomai?Siomai is often enjoyed with dipping sauces such as soy sauce mixed with calamansi juice or chili garlic sauce. It pairs well with rice or as part of a larger meal.
